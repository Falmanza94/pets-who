American Robin

This bird is typically seen hopping on the ground looking for juicy worms during the warmer seasons.

Bald Eagle

This bird builds the largest nest of any bird in the US, on average the size of a queen-sized bed.

Baltimore Oriole

This bird is Maryland's state bird and the name of professional baseball team within the state.

Barred Owl

Historians believe that Harriet Tubman used the call of this bird to signal people seeking to use the Underground Railroad.

Black-Capped Chickadee

This bird will hide food in various locations to snack on for later and can apparently remember thousands of hiding places.

Blue Jay

This bird often mimics the sound of the Red-shouldered Hawk either as a warning sign for other jays or maybe to trick others.

Canda Goose

This bird flies in a characteristic V-shape while in migration to help maintain the flock's energy and assist one another in communicating.

Carolina Wren

This noisy little bird was once recorded to have sung nearly 3,000 times in one day!

Cedar Waxwing

This little bird can sometimes become intoxicated from eating too many overripe berries, its main source of food.

Common Grackle

This bird is the #1 threat to corn in the United States of America! DUN DUN DUUUUUN!

Cooper's Hawk

Once known for primarily hunting in forests, this bird is now commonly seen hunting small songbirds in urban and suburban areas.

Eastern Bluebird

This bird is the Missouri and New York state bird and is also a symbol of hope, love, and renewal.

Great Egret

This bird is the symbol of the Audubon Society and is sometimes referred to as the angel bird.

Indigo Bunting

This bird migrates at night using the stars as a guide, even through the changing orientation as the night progresses.

Mourning Dove

This bird remains one of the most abundant birds in the US with a population of ~350 million.

Northern Flicker

This bird's tongue is attached to a specialized bone which wraps around the skull and acts as an extender and shock absorber!
